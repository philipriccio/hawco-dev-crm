## Strategic Framework

**Philip's co-pro test (non-negotiable):**
The story must authentically belong to both countries. Either the narrative spans both geographies, or the lead character moves from one to the other. Token settings don't qualify and won't survive broadcaster scrutiny.

**Priority treaty partners for Hawco:**

| Country | Why | Key Broadcasters |
|---------|-----|-----------------|
| **UK** | Biggest upside. Shared language. Active co-pro market. | BBC One, ITV, Channel 4, Sky Atlantic, UKTV |
| **Ireland** | Punching above its weight right now. Literary culture. | RTÉ, Virgin Media, Element Pictures |
| **Australia** | Crime/thriller on fire. Active treaty. English language. | ABC, Nine/Binge, Foxtel, Stan |
| **France** | Quality drama, prestige. ARTE is an aggressive co-pro partner. | France Télévisions, Canal+, ARTE |
| **Germany** | ARTE Germany. Strong in international drama. | ZDF, ARD, ARTE |

## SHORTLIST ASSESSMENT (from your research doc)

### 🟢 #1 PRIORITY: **419 — Will Ferguson (2012)**
**Giller Prize Winner**

**The story:** A Calgary father's daughter is killed after falling for a Nigerian advance-fee email scam. He pursues the scammer — the story cuts between Calgary, Lagos, and London.

**Co-pro case:**
This is the strongest co-pro candidate on the entire list. It is structurally a three-country story — Canada, Nigeria, UK. Calgary anchors the Canadian financing. Lagos is genuinely central to the plot, not incidental. London ties in the UK broadcaster angle (BBC, Channel 4). Nigeria is not a treaty country but that's irrelevant — what matters is the Canadian and UK story. The Lagos sequences give it international scale and visual ambition that will excite a prestige broadcaster.

**Rights status:** No adaptation found in 13 years. Early option almost certainly lapsed. This is a clean target.

**Comparable:** *Dirty Money* meets *McMafia*. BBC One/Channel 4 level drama.

**Agent:** Gráinne Fox, UTA — Grainne.Fox@unitedtalent.com

**Recommendation:** Approach immediately. This is the #1 target.

---

### 🟢 #2 PRIORITY: **The Boat People — Sharon Bala (2018)**
**Harper Lee Prize Winner**

**The story:** 492 Sri Lankan Tamil refugees arrive by freighter in Vancouver. Told from three perspectives — a refugee, a lawyer, a government adjudicator. A government determined to deport them. A community torn.

**Co-pro case:**
The refugee journey spans Sri Lanka and Canada. The UK angle: the moral and political debate at the centre of this story is as live in the UK and Australia as it is in Canada. The Sri Lanka sequences + the Vancouver courtroom drama = natural UK/Australia co-pro structure. This is a character-driven legal drama with urgent contemporary politics — exactly what BBC/Channel 4 and ABC Australia commission.

**Rights status:** No adaptation found. Appears clean.

**Comparable:** *The Good Wife* meets *Alias Grace*. Prestige drama, 6-8 episodes.

**Agent:** Transatlantic Agency — carolyn@transatlanticagency.com (Stephanie Sinclair has moved — contact agency directly)

**Recommendation:** Strong #2 target. Approach alongside 419.

---

### 🟡 #3 PRIORITY: **Never Coming Home — Hannah Mary McKinnon (2022)**
**ITW Award Finalist**

**The story:** A British sociopath murders his wealthy wife and tries to cover his tracks. Dark comedy, unreliable narrator, villain's POV.

**Co-pro case:**
The protagonist is British — this is an explicit Canada-UK story. The villain is from the UK, living in Canada. A British broadcaster co-producing a story about a British man's crimes in Canada is a natural sell. Darkly comic thriller in the vein of *Fleabag* meets *You*.

**Rights status:** McKinnon's bio says "work has been optioned" without specifying which title. Must verify before approaching. If *Never Coming Home* is clean, this is an excellent target.

**Agent:** Carolyn Forde, Transatlantic Agency — carolyn@transatlanticagency.com

**Recommendation:** Verify rights status first — single email to Carolyn Forde asking whether *Never Coming Home* specifically is available. If yes, pursue. If no, pivot to *Sister Dear* (same agent, likely clean).

---

### 🟡 #4: **Blackwater Falls — Ausma Zehanat Khan (2022)**
**Inaya Rahman Series #1**

**The story:** Pakistani Muslim detective Inaya Rahman investigates the murder of a Syrian refugee girl in a Colorado town. Police corruption, community fracture, systemic bias.

**Co-pro case:**
This one is more complex. The setting is Colorado (US), not Canada. The co-pro angle is thematic — diverse Muslim female lead, immigration/refugee themes — rather than geographic. This makes the co-pro pitch harder: you'd need a UK or Australian broadcaster who wants to make a US-set show, which is possible (see *The Americans* etc.) but less natural.

**Rights status:** Appears clean on the Inaya Rahman series — the previously optioned titles are the Khattak/Getty series via Lionsgate. Must confirm with agent.

**Recommendation:** Worth a call to verify, but lower priority than the top three. The lack of Canadian/UK geography weakens the co-pro case.

## TIER 1 ADDITIONAL RECOMMENDATIONS

### **What Strange Paradise — Omar El Akkad (2021)**
**Giller Prize Winner**

**The story:** A 9-year-old Syrian boy, the sole survivor of a refugee shipwreck on a Mediterranean island. Told in parallel — his journey and a local Greek girl who tries to help him escape.

**Co-pro case:**
Syria, the Mediterranean (Greece/European coastal), and Canada (El Akkad is Canadian). The story is genuinely international — it's not set in Canada at all, which may complicate Canadian financing, but also means it's a natural European co-pro (Greece, France, Germany via ARTE). This is prestige European-Canadian drama.

**Rights status:** No adaptation found despite 5 years and high profile. Appears clean.

**Recommendation:** Strong alternative. Could pair as a second title with 419 for a Banff pitch. More prestige/awards-oriented than commercial.

---

### **The Finder — Will Ferguson (2015)**
**Crime Writers of Canada Award Winner**

**The story:** A journalist investigates a wave of Indigenous youth suicides in remote Saskatchewan — drawn deeper into a conspiracy of silence.

**Co-pro case:**
Purely Canadian story. No natural international co-pro geography. Strong Canadian drama, strong social stakes — but the co-pro argument would have to rest on thematic universality rather than dual setting.

**Rights status:** Appears clean. Same agent as 419 (Gráinne Fox, UTA).

**Recommendation:** Lower priority for co-pro purposes. Could be developed as a domestic Canadian production. If you're already approaching Fox about 419, ask about The Finder in the same conversation — low-cost intelligence.

---

### **Things We Do in the Dark — Jennifer Hillier (2022)**
**ITW Award Winner**

**The story:** A Filipino-Canadian woman is accused of murdering her elderly celebrity husband in Toronto. The real mystery: the life she invented to escape her past.

**Co-pro case:**
Toronto setting gives it Canadian roots. The Filipino backstory gives it an international dimension — Philippines-Canada co-pro is less common but possible. Dark domestic thriller with class/celebrity themes. More UK-accessible than some others given the prestige-thriller tone.

**Rights status:** Hillier's bio says "work has been optioned" — unclear which title. Verify before approaching.

**Recommendation:** Verify rights status only. Don't invest until that's clear.
